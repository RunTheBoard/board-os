# Brand Research

Research and document brand positioning for strategic decisions.

## Context Needed

Before researching, read:
- `memory/MEMORY.md` — existing brand context, industry, competitors
- `memory/projects/` — any prior research or brand documentation
- Current brand assets or positioning statements if available

## Steps

1. **Define scope** — What aspect of brand positioning?
   - Competitive landscape
   - Target audience definition
   - Messaging/positioning gaps
   - Market opportunity
   - If unclear, ask: "What decision will this research support?"

2. **Research** using web search:
   - Competitor positioning (websites, social, ads)
   - Market trends in the industry
   - Audience demographics and behavior
   - Pricing landscape

3. **Analyze and synthesize:**
   - What's the whitespace? Where are competitors weak?
   - What does the target audience actually care about? (not what brands think they care about)
   - What positioning is already crowded?
   - Where can this brand credibly differentiate?

4. **Deliver a positioning recommendation:**
   - Core positioning statement (1 sentence)
   - Supporting proof points (3-5)
   - Messaging pillars (3)
   - What to avoid (competitor territory)

## Capture to Memory

After delivery:
- Save the full research to `memory/projects/brand-research-[date].md`
- Update `memory/MEMORY.md` with confirmed positioning decisions
- Note any competitor insights that should be tracked over time
- Record the operator's reactions to recommendations (what resonated, what didn't)
